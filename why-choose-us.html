What Sets Us Apart

Six pillars that have kept our customers returning — and referring — year after year.

25+ Years of Mastery

Our founder began sculpting Ganeshas in 1999. Today, three generations of artisans carry forward those techniques, refining them with every season of Ganesh Chaturthi.

100% Eco-Friendly Materials

We exclusively use natural Shadu clay sourced from the Godavari riverbed and plant-based pigments. Our idols dissolve naturally, leaving zero chemical residue in water bodies.

Every Piece is Unique

We do not mass-produce. Each idol is hand-sculpted, meaning no two are identical. Your Ganpati carries the subtle imprint of the artisan who created it — a living work of art.

Quality You Can See and Feel

Before any idol leaves our studio, it passes a 12-point quality inspection — checking proportions, paint adhesion, surface finish, and structural integrity.

Safe, Insured Delivery

Every idol is packed in custom foam-fitted boxes with shock-absorbing layers. We offer insured shipping across India and even international delivery on request.

Post-Purchase Care

We guide you on idol care, puja setup, and eco-friendly Visarjan. Our team is reachable even after the festival to help with any restoration or repainting needs.

Rooted in Tradition, Committed to the Planet

Long before eco-friendly became a trend, we were making clay idols the way our grandparents taught us — with river clay, natural ochre, and turmeric-based paints. We never switched to Plaster of Paris, even when it was cheaper and easier. Because we believe that what goes into the river on Visarjan day affects the fish, the water, and ultimately us all.

Today, we go further — partnering with NGOs to organise community Visarjan tanks, educating customers on proper idol immersion, and replanting trees to offset the clay extraction we use. When you choose Div Mangal Murtis, you are choosing a greener Ganesh Chaturthi.

Our Creation Process

From a blank lump of clay to a divine presence in your home — here is how we bring each Ganpati to life.

01

Understanding Your Vision

We begin with a detailed consultation — learning about the occasion, the space, your preferred pose (Baithi, Shayani, Nritya), and any personalisation you desire.

02

Clay Preparation

Natural Shadu clay is hand-kneaded and conditioned for 48 hours. This ensures the right plasticity for fine detailing without cracks during drying.

03

Hand Sculpting

Master sculptors shape the armature and build up layers of clay, carving intricate ornaments, crown details, and facial expressions by hand — no moulds, no shortcuts.

04

Drying and Firing

Idols are slow-dried in shade for 7–10 days, then sun-cured. This traditional process prevents cracking and ensures a rock-solid base for painting.

05

Natural Painting

Plant-based and mineral pigments are applied in multiple layers. Gold leaf or metallic accents are added by hand for a rich, vibrant finish that lasts the entire festival season.

06

Quality Check and Dispatch

A final 12-point inspection ensures perfection. The idol is then wrapped in soft fabric, nested in custom foam, and dispatched in a double-walled carton with live tracking.
